This story portrays the extraordinary life and inner reflections of a female monk who was born without a brain due to anencephaly, yet can now walk, speak, think, and feel emotions thanks to an advanced mechanical brain and artificial intelligence software. Throughout her childhood, she endured bullying and faced job discrimination despite her exceptional abilities. After deeply pondering the meaning of her existence, she ultimately chose to view her unique life as a blessing, dedicating herself as a monk to supporting children facing similar struggles.

In a quiet cathedral, she confesses:

"Isn't it a blessing that I, born with a body that should never have survived, am alive today?"

She prays that divine grace accompanies every child navigating their own extraordinary paths. This work delicately captures the beautiful and noble choices of a soul blossoming at the intersection of pain and blessing, technology and faith.
